Capital One, Rotation One


Tech Stack: Java (11) Spring, React.js, AWS

Team: Scantool - securing Capital One's entire data lake


My team was tasked with maintaining security within our entire data lake comprising of 20+ petabytes of data. This was achieved through scanning files as they landed in the lake, and re-scanning the entire data lake in set times during the year to maintain governance with constantly evolving policies and standards.

At our max, we can scan 1 PB per day. On a more normal basis, we scan roughly 300-400 terabytes a day.

AWS services I got familiar with: EC2, S3, RDS, SQS, Lambda, IAM, Cloudformation, CloudWatch.

AWS services I used briefly: DynamoDb, SNS.

Other tools I used: Jenkins, Terraform, Ansible, ELK, Docker, Snowflake.


Things I've done:


  • Implemented caching solution for operations reaching 5000 TPS, reducing millions of false positive in our scanning.
  • Implemented caching solution to alleviate RDS replicas during intensive workloads evaluating 40 million objects frequently.
  • Managed a team of 5 analysts focused on examining our scan results for true and false positives. Reported results to leadership to evaluate value.
  • Worked with adding custom features supporting our federated infrastructure, managed by toggling profiles. This allowed internal partners to run our tool suite for their own particular needs.
  • Quantified impacts of features developed through metrics integrated with Datadog.
  • Delievered 'data managers' that directly scanned hundreds of terabytes of data per day.
  • Created various REST APIs to fulfill our applications' needs.
  • Created components in our SPA React.js frontend.
  • Integrated with Snowflake to enable easy querying of our data.
  • Used Lambda + CloudWatch to assist with activating a custom graceful shutdown in our autoscaling policies.
  • Actively contribute to our team's support channels.
  • Volunteered to host lunch meetings for tech interviewees. :)
